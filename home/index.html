Just over 10% of all clothing donated is sold in thrift stores; the rest is either thrown in a troublingly massive pile in landfills or burned, adding to CO2 emissions.

A staggering amount of clothing is not sold at all. It varies by company; for example, H&M doesn't sell roughly $4.3 billion in clothing each year, hence approximately 10% of their stock goes straight to trash.

In the UK, each piece of clothing purchased is worn estimated 7 times before being discarded; however, in China, that number drops to as low as 3.

The fashion industry has produced more CO2 gases than the United Kingdom, France, and Germany combined, responsible for roughly 4% of total global emissions. Recycling can help change this, as material production accounts for nearly 40% of pollution.

Worse, despite companies changing their policies, newcomers take their place, projecting the fast-fashion industry to triple by 2050.
